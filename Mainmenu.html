Gujarat (Gujarati: ગુજરાત is a state on the western coast of India. It has the longest coastline in the country, stretching approximately 1,600 km (990 mi), much of which lies along the Kathiawar Peninsula. Covering an area of 196,024 km² (75,685 sq mi), Gujarat is the fifth-largest state in India and, as per the 2011 census, has a population of 60.4 million, making it the ninth-most populous. It shares borders with Rajasthan to the northeast, Madhya Pradesh to the east, Maharashtra to the southeast, and the union territories of Dadra and Nagar Haveli and Daman and Diu to the south. The Arabian Sea lies to its west, along with the Pakistani province of Sindh. Gandhinagar serves as the capital, while Ahmedabad is the largest city and a major economic hub. Gujarati, the official language, is spoken by the state's indigenous people, the Gujaratis.

Gujarat is home to 23 sites of the ancient Indus Valley Civilization, the most among any Indian state. Notable sites include Lothal, believed to have housed the world's first dry dock and one of the earliest seaports, Dholavira, the fifth-largest Indus Valley site, and Gola Dhoro, where unique seals were discovered. Historically, coastal cities like Bharuch and Khambhat were prominent trade centers during the Maurya and Gupta empires, as well as under the rule of the Western Satraps.

The state is among the four in India, alongside Bihar, Mizoram, and Nagaland, where alcohol sale is prohibited. Gujarat is also known for the Gir Forest National Park, which is home to the world’s only wild population of Asiatic lions.

Economically, Gujarat is one of India's most industrialized states, contributing significantly to national exports, accounting for nearly one-third of India's total. It has the fifth-largest economy among Indian states, with a Gross State Domestic Product (GSDP) of ₹16.55 trillion (approximately US$210 billion in 2023). The state's per capita GSDP is ₹215,000 (US$2,500). Despite its economic strength and low unemployment rate, Gujarat ranks 21st in the Human Development Index (HDI) and has faced challenges related to social indicators and occasional religious tensions.
